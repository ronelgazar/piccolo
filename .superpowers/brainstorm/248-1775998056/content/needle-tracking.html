How should we detect and track the needle?

The needle's visual characteristics will determine the best tracking method. Pick the approach that best matches what you see in the video.

A

Color Segmentation (HSV)

Best if the needle has a distinct color (metallic silver, dark, or bright) that stands out from the white grid background. Fast and robust for high-contrast needles.

Pros

  • Very fast
  • Simple to tune
  • Works well on clean backgrounds

Cons

  • Sensitive to lighting changes
  • Fails if color is similar to grid
B

Edge + Contour Detection

Best if the needle is thin and elongated (like a surgical needle or pin). Uses Canny edges and finds the needle by its shape — long, thin, pointed contour.

Pros

  • Works regardless of needle color
  • Good for metallic needles
  • Tracks tip precisely

Cons

  • Grid lines may interfere
  • Needs shape filtering
C

Background Subtraction + Blob

Best if the grid is static and only the needle moves. Subtracts the background to isolate movement, then tracks the moving blob (needle tip).

Pros

  • Ignores static grid automatically
  • Very clean isolation of movement
  • Works even if needle blends with grid

Cons

  • Requires stable camera
  • Noise from vibration
